GIRAFFE

The giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis) is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant on Earth. Native to Africa, giraffes are easily recognized by their extremely long necks and legs, unique coat patterns, and ossiconesâ€”horn-like structures on their heads.

Adult males can reach heights of up to 5.5 meters (18 feet), while females are slightly shorter. Their long necks allow them to feed on leaves, fruits, and flowers from tall trees, especially acacias, which are inaccessible to most other herbivores.

Giraffes are social animals, often found in loose groups without a strong social bond. Despite their size, they can run at speeds of up to 60 km/h (37 mph) for short distances. Their height gives them excellent visibility to spot predators like lions from afar.
