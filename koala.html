KOALA

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus) is a tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia, recognized for its fluffy ears, spoon-shaped nose, and gentle expression. Often called a "koala bear" (incorrectly, as they are not bears), koalas are one of Australia's most iconic animals.

Koalas feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which are low in nutrients and toxic to most animals. To cope with this diet, koalas have a specialized digestive system and sleep up to 18â€“22 hours a day to conserve energy. Their strong limbs and sharp claws make them excellent climbers.

Koalas are generally solitary and communicate using loud bellows, especially during mating season. Habitat destruction, bushfires, and disease (such as chlamydia) are major threats to their survival in the wild.
