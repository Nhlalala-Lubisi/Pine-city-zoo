PANDAS

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is a bear species native to the mountain forests of central China. Known for its distinctive black-and-white coat and gentle demeanor, the panda has become a global symbol of wildlife conservation.

Pandas are classified as carnivores, but their diet is over 99% bamboo. They spend up to 14 hours a day eating, using their strong jaws and pseudo-thumbs to grasp and chew bamboo stalks. Occasionally, they may eat other plants or even small animals.

Giant pandas are solitary and spend most of their time alone. They have a highly developed sense of smell that allows them to avoid other individuals and locate potential mates. Pandas are currently classified as vulnerable, with habitat loss and low reproduction rates being their main threats.
