WARTHOGS

Warthogs (Phacochoerus africanus) are wild members of the pig family native to Africa. Known for their large curved tusks, wart-like facial bumps, and sparse body hair, warthogs are surprisingly fast and intelligent animals that thrive in savannahs, grasslands, and woodlands.

They use their tusks for defense and digging, and often kneel on their front knees while feeding on grasses, roots, and bulbs. Despite their tough appearance, warthogs are primarily herbivores but will scavenge if the opportunity arises.

Warthogs are social creatures, typically seen in small groups called sounders, which consist of females and their young. Males are more solitary. When threatened, warthogs run with their tails held upright as a warning signal for others.
