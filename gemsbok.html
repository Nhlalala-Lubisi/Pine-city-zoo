GEMSBOK

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope native to arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. It is known for its striking appearance, with long, straight horns and bold black-and-white facial markings. Gemsbok are part of the Oryx genus, which also includes other desert-dwelling antelope species.

They are well-adapted to dry conditions, capable of going without water for extended periods by extracting moisture from the plants they eat. Their light-colored coats reflect sunlight, and they are most active during cooler parts of the day.

Both males and females have horns, which can grow up to 85 cm (33 in) long and are used for defense against predators and during fights for dominance. Their strong build and horns help them deter threats such as lions and hyenas.
