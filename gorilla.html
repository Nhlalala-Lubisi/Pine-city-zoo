GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest living primates and are native to the forests of central Sub-Saharan Africa. There are two species: the eastern gorilla (Gorilla beringei) and the western gorilla (Gorilla gorilla), each with two subspecies. Gorillas share 98â€“99% of their DNA with humans, making them one of our closest relatives in the animal kingdom.

They are known for their muscular build, broad chests, and gentle nature. Gorillas live in groups called troops or bands led by a dominant male known as a silverback, named for the distinctive silver hair on his back. They primarily eat leaves, fruit, stems, and bamboo shoots.

Despite their powerful appearance, gorillas are shy and peaceful animals. They communicate through vocalizations, body postures, and facial expressions. Sadly, gorillas are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and diseases.
